About I Want That\!

I started working with retailers in 1998\.  I ran database marketing operations for a $17 billion retail conglomerate, 12 retail brands, 2 billion sku item transactions, 85 million consumers.  You learn a few things along the way.

One key insight:

20% of buyers pay full markup \- time is more valuable than a discount  
40% of buyers pay 80% of markup \- they take deals when they can get them  
40% of buyers cannot buy without a discount \- you are simply out of reach from them

That was not the insight. The insight was how to profit in various ways from all of them. Billion dollar brands use allowances in their pricing, a deliberate structure to facilitate scaling.  20% pay full markup, 40% partial and 40% need a lot of help.  The allowances produce a shield to flex your terms and price points for a wider array of buyers.  Allowances are the key to price elasticity of demand \- the higher unit volume at lower price and still being profitable.

The current economic model in direct-to-consumer retail is rigged against you.  Everyone wants a percent of your sales.  When you scale up they “share” in the growth.  But what really happens is your costs scale up and offset your revenues. We do not do that.  Ever. We use flat recurring subscriptions so you can scale millions of dollars over small, smart investments.

And the CAC model is only broken because you are getting half of the equation.  Your ads get buyers, but they do not convert.  We layer in making offers to convert more buyers and drive your CAC through the floor. Stop leaving money on the table.

My retail baby was Bryden Road, for people who work from home, accessories and decor.  10 years before it’s time.  I shut it down when my consulting took over my life.  And when I sold my merchandise on eBay, I made higher margins with Make an Offer than I did guessing what a customer would take.  The person who knows the best offer for them is them.  

Enter I Want That\!  A fun way to engage customers, have them make offers to you to buy their carts, and reveal their price points and unit volumes along the way.  A business built by a retailer for retailers to help you grow, and grow profitably.
