# What is the Financing Allowance?

## Definition

The Financing Allowance is the markup buffer that absorbs the cost of capital embedded in inventory between purchase and sale. It is Allowance 2 of The Five Allowances and funds the time cost of holding inventory.

## How the Financing Allowance works

Inventory ties up cash. Whether the operator funds inventory through debt, a line of credit, investor capital, or retained earnings, that capital carries an economic cost.

The Financing Allowance prices that cost into markup. The longer inventory sits unsold, the more financing pressure it creates.

Operators often underestimate this allowance because the financing cost is not attached directly to the SKU. Instead, it appears in debt service, opportunity cost, or working capital pressure elsewhere in the business.

## Why the Financing Allowance matters in 2026

Interest rates increased materially after 2021, raising the effective carrying cost of inventory across ecommerce and retail.

At the same time, slower inventory turns in many categories extended the average holding period for goods. Together, these changes dramatically increased the financing burden embedded inside inventory.

Operators still pricing inventory using pre-2021 financing assumptions are frequently underfunding markup.

## How the Financing Allowance differs from Profit Markup

Profit Markup funds the operation and intended profitability of the business.

The Financing Allowance funds the time cost of inventory capital itself. It exists before profit is realized and regardless of whether the inventory ultimately sells profitably.

## How to apply the Financing Allowance to your store

1. **Estimate the blended cost of inventory capital** across credit facilities, retained cash, and investor capital.
2. **Measure average inventory holding periods** by category or SKU group.
3. **Fund the allowance into markup** based on expected inventory duration and carrying cost.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the Financing Allowance in retail pricing?**  
A: The Financing Allowance is the pricing buffer that absorbs the cost of capital tied up in inventory between purchase and sale.

**Q: Why did the Financing Allowance become more important after 2021?**  
A: Rising interest rates and slower inventory turns significantly increased the carrying cost of inventory across ecommerce and retail.

**Q: Is the Financing Allowance only relevant for debt-funded inventory?**  
A: No. Even inventory funded with retained cash carries opportunity cost and capital exposure. All inventory has a financing cost.
